HCSL Research

HCSL research notes provide concise conceptual introductions to the principal research topics addressed by HCSL.

Statistical Quality Control Design and Optimization

Statistical quality control evaluates whether an analytical process conforms to predefined quality specifications. The note discusses optimization approaches, including bioinspired methods such as genetic algorithms and neural networks.

Research Note: Quality Control Design and Optimization

Reliability and Risk Based Quality Control

Risk based optimization of statistical quality control treats analytical performance as a probabilistic assessment problem involving reliability, risk, and measurement uncertainty.

Research Note: Reliability and Risk Based Quality Control

Medical Diagnosis

Medical diagnosis may be threshold-based, assessed through diagnostic accuracy measures, or supported by empirical or formal Bayesian methods.

Research Note: Medical Diagnosis

Uncertainty of Diagnostic Measures

Estimation and evaluation of uncertainty in diagnostic measures are central to medical diagnosis and to the definition of analytical performance specifications.

Research Note: Uncertainty of Diagnostic Measures
