About Us

Hellenic Complex Systems Laboratory

Hellenic Complex Systems Laboratory (HCSL) is a virtual research laboratory devoted to the transdisciplinary study of complex systems. Founded in 1993 in Drama, Eastern Macedonia and Thrace, Greece, it has been owned and operated by the Chatzimichail family since then.

Legal Basis

Hellenic Complex Systems Laboratory exists as an unincorporated association, as defined in Conservative and Unionist Central Office v. Burrell (1982) 55 TC 671 (UK).

Logotype

  Copyright © 1993, by A. T. Chatzimichail, HCSL. All rights reserved.

The Hellenic Complex Systems Laboratory logotype features four symmetrical, interlaced Archimedean spirals, symbolizing the interconnectedness and emergent properties inherent in complex systems.

Motto

The Hellenic Complex Systems Laboratory motto, 'All is one' (Heraclitus, On the Universe, I ), encapsulates the foundational philosophy of unity and interconnectedness underlying complex systems.

The standard ELOT 743 (identical to the ISO 843) is currently being applied to transliterate the Hellenic names into Latin characters. Accordingly, the Hellenic family name Χατζημιχαήλ is transliterated into Chatzimichail instead of Hatjimihail.
